"Hello, World!" For many, these two simple words represent the beginning of a journey. It's the traditional first program that aspiring programmers write when learning a new language, a rite of passage that signifies entry into the world of coding. More than just a simple print statement, "Hello, World!" embodies the fundamental principles of syntax, compilation, and execution. This article delves into the significance of this iconic program, exploring its history, purpose, and enduring relevance in the ever-evolving landscape of software development.
